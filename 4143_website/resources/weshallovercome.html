Why This Source

One of the most recognized songs during the Civil Rights movement, "We Shall Overcome" has a rich history with an interesting twist.

The song fits into quite a few themes from class.

The first source, from the Library of Congress, provides a wonderful historical overview, with the source from the Atlantic adding a coda that is relevant to class.

Summary

"We Shall Overcome" is rooted in I'll Overcome Some Day and I'll be All Right, both rooted in African American culture.

Interviews were also recorded with Pete Seeger and Jamila Jones. Copyright was made by Pete Seeger, Guy Carawan and Frank Hamilton. In 1956 at age 12, Jamila Jones added a new verse to "We Shall Overcome."

After Kate Stewart published her article in 2014, it was discovered that a woman by the name of Louise Shropshire wrote a hymn called "If My Jesus Wills" which was claimed to be direct inspiration for "We Shall Overcome".

The copyright to "We Shall Overcome" was challenged in court and voluntarily relinquished by its publishers in 2018 after historical evidence, including Shropshire's hymn, demonstrated the song's status as a traditional folk work in the public domain.

Critical Analysis

Heroification

Pete Seeger is rightly upheld as a pillar of integrity and solidarity. Yet even our heroes of any movement are not perfect, complicated by flaws and controversies, such as the disputes surrounding the copyright and attribution for "We Shall Overcome."

Power

The transformative power of song is central, as "We Shall Overcome" was powerfully embraced by the Civil Rights Movement, demonstrating how music can fuel and sustain social change.

Resistance

The song was inherently created and adopted as an anthem of protest, a direct musical tool for challenging injustice and asserting collective strength.

Solidarity

Music like "We Shall Overcome" plays a crucial role in building solidarity, helping to unify diverse individuals within a movement around a shared identity and common purpose.

Humanity

The emphatic "We" in "We Shall Overcome" underscores a fundamental theme: that humanity is not a solitary experience but an interconnected community facing struggles together.

What Does It Mean to Be American?

The song raises core American questions: Who is the "we" that shall overcome? What systemic obstacles must they overcome? And what does their struggle reveal about the nation's ideals and realities?

Erasure

While often unintentional, a notable erasure occurred in the historical narrative of "We Shall Overcome," where a key contributor, Louise Shropshire, was overlooked for decades.

Reframing/Reclaiming

Recent historical revelations about the song's origins have allowed for a reframing of its story, reclaiming the credit and recognition.

Omitted Voices/Stories/Perspectives

The erasure of certain creators points to the broader omission of vital voices and perspectives in popular history, a gap that reclaiming efforts seek to address.

Silent Conversation

Class activity

Summary

Since the "We Shall Overcome" resource has many videos within the various references, as well as plenty of shorter readings on the various aspects and history of the song, students have the ability to intellectually roam around.

Creating pods, students go from one pod to another, which has a resouce--either a video playing on a laptop, a short reading or photos--as well as a blank piece of paper for them to write down their thoughts.

After a given amount of time, the students move on to another pod, absorb the material and write down their thoughts.

Critical Analysis for Class Activity

Omitted voices/stories/perspectives

Part of the inspiration for this is that Louise Shropshire did not receive proper credit for her contribution to the song. Having the students participate via silent conversation will hopefull connect some dots.

Dramatization

We should consider if this is dramatization. I would say no, as this connection will not be explicitly made. Perhaps students will notice during a debrief. However, since silent conversation is an establsiehd classroom activity, I would not consider it a gimmick here.
