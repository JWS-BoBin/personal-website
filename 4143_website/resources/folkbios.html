Folk Bios

Dicaire, D. (2010). The early years of folk music. McFarland & Company.

Dicaire, D. (2011). The folk music revival, 1958-1970. McFarland & Company.

Why This Source

Dave Dicaire's books, The early years of folk music and The folk music revival, 1958-1970, provide wonderful short profiles on many folk musicians and other notable figures in folk music revival. Well-researched, this offers a great way to diversify and widen the range of artists discussed in the study of folk music.

Summary

These thorough references by music enthusiast David Dicaire profile numerous folk musicians from various backgrounds and eras. A notable strength is the inclusion of international folk singers, expanding the scope beyond America.

Critical Analysis

Windows and Mirrors

Allows students to read about a wide range of folk musicians, many of which come from different backgrounds, some of which can come from their own unique backgrounds.

Omitted Stories

The books bring to the fore many singers that may not be known to a wider audience, allowing these artists to reclaim their position in the history of folk and culture.

Jigsaw

Class activity

Summary

Small groups will be given bios of various folk artisits from the Dicaire (2010, 2011) books.

They will then get into groups to discuss their subjects.

They will then jigsaw, so that everyone has the opportunity to share their subject with members of every other group.
